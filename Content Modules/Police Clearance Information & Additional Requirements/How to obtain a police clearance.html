Some organizations that work with vulnerable populations require volunteers to obtain a vulnerable sector police clearance or criminal record check before they can begin volunteering. This requirement will be stated in the position description on mysuccess (near the bottom). 

It can take 1-3 weeks* to obtain a police clearance from Windsor Police Services. It is your responsibility to apply promptly so you can start volunteering as soon as possible.  

If you live or rent in Windsor, you would obtain a police check from Windsor Police Services. If you live outside of Windsor you would contact your local law enforcement office. For example, in Lakeshore, it is the OPP office located in Belle River.  The process varies between areas, call and find out what you need to do to apply for a clearance if you cannot find the necessary information on their website. Most places let you apply online and that can speed up the process.

As of April 2022 Criminal Record Checks for volunteers are free (there may be a service charge if you apply online). Vulnerable Sector Checks are not free, check the police website for current cost. Vulnerable Sector Checks are usually what VIP partners ask for.

You will need a letter from the organization you are matched with to submit in order to apply for a vulnerable sector check. As soon as you are matched, ask for this letter and then apply immediately for your clearance. Keep your receipt to show when you applied. You may or may not need a letter to obtain a Criminal Record Check, it depends on the police service.
