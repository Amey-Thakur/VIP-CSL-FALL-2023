Many organizations will ask you for references. Everything on your resume can be proven by contacting your references. A reference is a person who can speak to your work ethic, skills, abilities, and characteristics. This usually means a former supervisor, manager, client, or colleague. If you do not have any work or volunteer experience you could use an academic reference. Do not use friends or family members. To be well prepared, bring a sheet with three references to your first meeting with your community partner supervisor just in case they ask for it. References should not be included on your resume. 

Here's what to include on a reference sheet for each reference:

  • Full Name of Person and their Job Title (e.g. Virginia Harrison, Store Manager)
  • Name of Organization with city and province (e.g. Home Hardware, Burlington ON)
  • Phone number and email address (e.g. 426-534-7899, vharrison@homehardware.com)
  • Explain the relationship to you (e.g. Former supervisor)
